I produce some copies of myself after 10 time units.

Begin micro-behaviour

ADD-SOME-OFFSPRING

Begin NetLogo code: 
do-after 
   [add-copies random-integer-between   [AGE-IS-ZERO UPDATE-MINIMUM-REPRODUCTION-AGE]]
End NetLogo code

Variants

You can change the number (or range of random numbers) of copies made by editing the text areas. The additional behaviours (here there is one that sets the age to zero and another that resets the reproduction age). You can change the 10 time units it waits before creating the offspring.

Related Micro-behaviours

This relies upon AGE-IS-ZERO and UPDATE-MINIMUM-REPRODUCTION-AGE. REPRODUCE uses this micro-behaviour.

A simpler example can be found in ADD-COPIES.

How this works

After 10 time units it creates a number of copies determined by the expression 'random-integer-between 1 3' which produces 1, 2, or 3 with equal probability.

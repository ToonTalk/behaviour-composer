I repeatedly select a random acquaintance and change my opinion accordingly. This implements part of the model in [1].

Begin micro-behaviour

CHANGE-OPINION-SOCIAL-NETWORK

Begin NetLogo code: 
do-every delta-t
   [set-the-other one-of my-acquaintances
    add-behaviour RELATIVE-AGREEMENT
    add-behaviour-to the-other RELATIVE-AGREEMENT]
End NetLogo code

After every delta-t then I run the RELATIVE-AGREEMENT micro-behaviour with a randomly selected acquaintance which may change my opinion. My acquaintance also runs this micro-behaviour.

Variants

To see a log of the changes in opinion and uncertainty copy and paste the following at the end: 

 create-output-area 624 10 1246 546 ; create an area for the log
 output-print (word "self: " who " opinion: " my-opinion " next-opinion: " my-next-opinion)
 output-print (word "other: " who-of the-other " uncertainty: " my-uncertainty)

Related Micro-behaviours

CHANGE-OPINION-IN-NEIGHBOURHOOD differs from this in that the interaction is with someone located spatially nearby rather than an acquaintance anywhere in the model. This relies upon RELATIVE-AGREEMENT and INITIALISE-SOCIAL-NETWORK-CONSTANT-ASYMMETRIC.

This micro-behaviour is superseded by CREATE-NEW-AGENTS-IN-LOWER-LEFT-CORNER that supports experimentation with the population via a slider.

I create copies of myself at setup. Each copy has the additional behaviour of jumping to a random unoccupied position in the lower left corner of Sugarscape. Also various attributes are initialised by the INITIALISE-AGENT micro-behaviour.

Begin micro-behaviour

CREATE-INITIAL-POPULATION-IN-LOWER-LEFT-CORNER

End NetLogo code

Variants

Replace the 399 by any positive integer to make a different number of copies. In [1] the number is changed to 249 when the replacement rule (implemented by REPLACE-DEAD-AGENT) is introduced. (If you change the 399 to another number also change the name of the behaviour to avoid confusion.) You can change the list of behaviours that each newly created copy has. In this example there are just two micro-behaviours: SET-RANDOM-UNOCCUPIED-POSITION-IN-LOWER-LEFT-CORNER and INITIALISE-AGENT. You can create copies at any time, not just during setup time.

Related Micro-behaviours

CREATE-INITIAL-POPULATION distributes the initial population randomly over the entire Sugarscape.

How this works

This results in 400 copies (the "original" plus the 399 new ones) that are identical clones except for the changes caused by SET-RANDOM-UNOCCUPIED-POSITION-IN-LOWER-LEFT-CORNER and INITIALISE-AGENT.
